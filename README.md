# CS230_Portfolio
Portfolio assignment for CS 230 Week 8

The Gaming Room is a video game company client that owns an IP for "Draw it or Lose it".
This video game was originally designed as a mobile app. the client wanted to migrate the game
to a more accessible, web-based framework. The networking model required was a server-client setup.
I think I did well in the recommendation section of the documentation. The stark differences in use cases and 
cost of operating platforms in the industry made it very clear. Linux seems to be the solution for most requirements.
The most important and helpful parts of the deisgn document was the design constraints section. It allows for a freedom
of approach to the problem but with restrictions where the clients needs can be appropriately met. However, 
I would choose to revise the Domain model. I think it could have been simplified further and the singleton pattern of 
development could need more applicability in this case.
I considered the user's need by trying to fit the solution to the lowest cost, expediency, and minimum overhead of resources required. Its critically important since it directly affects the business relationship between the client and developer in the form of reliance and trust in providing an appropriate product.
I used the approach of breaking the problem down to conceptual units and subdividing them further until the most
apparent and obvious solutions existed for the simplest elements. I'd definitely use this method for all my future applications
till I find something more optimal.
